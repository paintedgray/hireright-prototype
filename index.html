Hiring Workflow Prototype

Problem

Recruiters reviewing 50–100 applicants must produce a defensible shortlist of 5–10 — at speed, with audit-grade reasoning, while retaining ownership.

Three things break this:

  • Compressed scoring — many candidates look similarly strong
  • Defensibility under audit — legal exposure if reasoning isn't captured
  • Manual override — recruiters add nuance the model can't see

Approach

  • The system always produces a defensible shortlist of top candidates, with statistical-tie clusters surfaced inside the list — so recruiters get a proper shortlist that they can work with.
  • Let recruiters define role criteria in a structured builder. The system will then enforce this consistently across every applicant during ranking.
  • Every time a recruiter disagrees with the model, the reason is captured from a curated taxonomy. As we get more overrides, the model learns from this data and becomes more valuable over time.
  • To make every decision audit-defensible, we should have pre-flight bias checks, override logs, criteria-change history, and reasons produced as a byproduct of the workflow.
