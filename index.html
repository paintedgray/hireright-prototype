Hiring Workflow Prototype

Problem

Recruiters reviewing 50–100 applicants must produce a defensible shortlist of 5–10 — at speed, with audit-grade reasoning, while retaining ownership.

Three things break this:

  • Compressed scoring — many candidates look similarly strong
  • Defensibility under audit — legal exposure if reasoning isn't captured
  • Manual override — recruiters add nuance the model can't see
Approach
  • Commit to a top-N. Always produce a defensible shortlist. Surface compression inside it as statistical-tie clusters.
  • Recruiter overrides are the moat. Capture override reasons as structured data, not free text. Day 1 we're comparable to off-the-shelf; Day 365 we're not.
  • Bias is managed, not avoided. Control runs upstream of the ranker, at the criteria-definition surface.
Goals
  • Define criteria with a hidden-bias check that runs before save
  • Always commit to top 5–10, with confidence bands shown honestly inside the commitment
  • Capture why recruiters override using a curated structured taxonomy
  • Log every decision in audit-grade detail
  • Catch bias upstream, not after the shortlist
